# Research Card: Commodity Supply Shock

## Hypothesis
Geopolitical supply disruptions (especially Strait of Hormuz / Middle East) create persistent upward trends in energy commodities and cross-asset effects (gold up, equities down, energy stocks up) that are tradeable via breakout and momentum signals.

## Economic Mechanism
- Physical supply removal creates genuine scarcity (not sentiment-driven)
- Strategic petroleum reserves provide temporary buffer but cannot fully offset major disruptions
- Supply shocks are persistent (months) vs demand shocks which are transient (Kilian 2009)
- Speculative flows amplify fundamental supply/demand imbalance (Singleton 2014)

## Academic Sources
1. Kilian (2009) — "Not All Oil Price Shocks Are Alike", AER
2. Hamilton (2003) — "What Is an Oil Price Shock?", Journal of Econometrics
3. Baumeister & Kilian (2016) — "Forty Years of Oil Price Fluctuations", JEP
4. Ready (2018) — "Oil Prices and the Stock Market", RFS
5. Singleton (2014) — "Investor Flows and the 2008 Boom/Bust in Oil Prices", Management Science

## Target Instruments
- Primary: USOIL.cash, UKOIL.cash
- Secondary: NATGAS.cash, HEATOIL.c
- Cross-asset: XAUUSD (long), XOM/CVX (long energy equities), US500 (short)
- Agricultural: WHEAT.c (if shipping lanes disrupted)

## Holding Period
- 5-30 trading days (supply disruption duration)

## Signal
- 10-day or 20-day Donchian breakout on crude oil
- Confirmation: spread between Brent and WTI widening (supply disruption affects Brent more)
- Cross-asset confirmation: gold rising AND equity indices falling
- COT overlay: commercials net long oil = additional conviction

## Position Sizing
- 0.3-0.5% risk per commodity trade (commodities are volatile)
- Max 4 commodity positions = 1.2-2.0% total risk
- Leave room for cross-asset trades (gold, equity short)

## Known Failure Modes
- Supply shock resolved quickly (2019 Aramco: oil reversed fully in 2 weeks)
- Strategic petroleum reserve release dampens price impact
- Global demand destruction from recession offsets supply shock
- Extreme intraday volatility causes stop-outs before trend resumes
- FTMO spread widening during extreme commodity vol

## FTMO Feasibility
- **MDL (3%)**: MODERATE RISK — oil can move 5-10% in a single day during supply shocks. Even correct directional bets can have intraday P&L swings of 2-3%.
- **Best Day Rule**: RISK — a 10% oil move could produce a single day that dominates total profits. Scale out on extreme days.
- **Weekend gap**: HIGH RISK — geopolitical developments over weekends cause commodity gaps. Cut positions by 50-75% on Friday.
- **Spread/swap**: Oil swaps are negative for longs. Budget accordingly for holds > 3 days.
- **Leverage**: Commodities at 10x leverage. 1 lot USOIL = $80k notional, margin ~$8k. Size carefully.

## Falsification Criteria
- Oil fails to break out above 20-day high within 5 days of confirmed supply disruption
- Supply shock produces < 10% oil price increase (too small to overcome transaction costs)
- FTMO simulation shows MDL breach rate > 20% due to intraday commodity volatility
